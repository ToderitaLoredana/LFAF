## Theory

Finite automata (FA) are models of computation used to recognise regular languages. A nondeterministic finite automaton (NDFA or NFA) may have several possible next states for a given state and input symbol, while a deterministic finite automaton (DFA) has at most one. The subset-construction (powerset) algorithm converts any NFA into an equivalent DFA by treating each DFA state as a set of NFA states.

Regular grammars (right- or left-linear) generate regular languages and are equivalent in expressive power to finite automata. Chomsky hierarchy classifies grammars into four types: Type-3 (regular), Type-2 (context-free), Type-1 (context-sensitive), and Type-0 (unrestricted).
